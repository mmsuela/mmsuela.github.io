Hi, I'm Marci.

I like to eat ice cream with chopsticks and I love newspapers. I consider myself to be a journalist, graphic designer, illustrator and just about everything in between. While I’ve worked on content for every section of the newspaper, my current focus is on diversity and lifestyle stories, including one about how people of color experience imposter syndrome.

Starting at Contra Costa Community College’s The Advocate, I won two Associated Collegiate Press Pacemakers in addition to a host of national and state individual awards. The storytelling and design skills led me to San Jose State University’s Spartan Daily, where I got to work overhauling the entire design and revamping the editorial process; I even spent a semester running the advertising team. My influence led to the Daily’s first-ever ACP Pacemaker finalist and being recognized as the 2019 best California college newspaper.
